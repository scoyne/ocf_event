Festival Entertainer

Learn more about the entertainers performing live at the Ohio Celtice Festival.

Boxing Banjo

Boxing Banjo is an Irish music band from the west coast of Ireland that plays a variety of instruments including the button accordion, tenor banjo, guitar, fiddle, and bodhrán. The band members are brothers Darragh and Micheal Healy (accordion and banjo), Joseph McNulty (fiddle), and Seán O'Meara (guitar). The four musicians have won more than 20 All Ireland titles and four world bodhrán titles, and have toured internationally.

Some say Boxing Banjo's music is "full steam ahead, sucking diesel, fueled by red-hot banjo and pumping box". Others say they are "complete naturals on stage".


Chelsea House Orchestra

The Chelsea House Orchestra (CHO) is a non-profit educational group of high school students from Chelsea High School in Michigan who perform a variety of music, mainly from Celtic sources. The CHO's arrangements are mostly melody-based, with little inner harmony or separate part writing, to allow everyone in the group to enjoy the music.


Crossbow

In 2011, Steve and Carly, along with vocalist/guitarist, Clare McMillan, founded CrossBow in a small music practice room. With just two violins and a guitar, the three young musicians never expected to showcase their traditional Irish music much beyond campus hallways and local pubs.


Barleyjuice

Formed originally in 1998, the group began as a side project for musicians Keith Swanson and Kyf Brewer, who met as bagpipers in the Loch Rannoch Pipes & Drums of Pineville, Pennsylvania.

Barleyjuice's focus was to perform traditional Irish and Scottish songs in a pop/rock fashion.


The Devil's Brigade

Drawing favourable comparisons to Wolfstone, Afro Celt Sound System, and Trans-Siberian Orchestra and consistently providing performances from Grade 1 bagpipers and other world class musicians this high-energy Canadian/American group has electrified audiences on three continents for years. They have expanded their ever-growing family of fans at music festivals, theatres, military bases, weddings, memorial ceremonies, corporate events, and more.


The Larkin and Moran Brothers

The Larkin and Moran Brothers are an Irish folk band from the Chicago area that are known for their energy and sense of fun. They are considered one of the most popular Irish/Celtic music bands in the Midwest.


Trish O'Brien

Trish has a rich history of live performance, recording and acting for which she has won many awards in Europe. Growing up in the city of Cork in her native Ireland, Trish learned the extreme highs and lows of the music and theatre industry through her parents. Her dad was the famed Brendan O'Brien of The Dixies - one of Ireland's top show-bands in the '60s and '70s, and her mum was a much-loved actress in local theatre productions. Music and theatre in your veins is a hard energy to ignore, so Trish chose to embrace her love and talent for both once moving to live in Canada.

During her career in Ireland, Trish not only won many national and provincial awards, but she also performed and hosted television shows broadcast across the entire nation.
